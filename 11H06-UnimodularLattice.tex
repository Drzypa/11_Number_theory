A lattice $L$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ is unimodular if a \PMlinkname{fundamental domain}{MinkowskisTheorem} of $L$ has volume 1.  This is \PMlinkescapetext{equivalent} to $L$ being unimodular as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module with the bilinear form \PMlinkescapetext{induced} from $\mathbb{R}^n$.
