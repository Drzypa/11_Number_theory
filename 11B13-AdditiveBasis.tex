A subset $A$ of $\mathbb{Z}$ is an \emph{(\PMlinkescapetext{additive}) basis} of \PMlinkescapetext{order} $n$ if
\begin{equation*}
nA=\mathbb{N}\cup\{0\},
\end{equation*}
where $nA$ is $n$-fold sumset of $A$. Usually it is assumed that $0$ belongs to $A$ when saying that $A$ is an additive basis.
