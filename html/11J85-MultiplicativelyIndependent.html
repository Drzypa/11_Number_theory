multiplicatively independent


A set X of nonzero complex numbers is said to be multiplicatively independent iff every equation

x1ν1⁢x2ν2⁢⋯⁢xnνn= 1

with  x1,x2,…,xn∈X  and  ν1,ν2,…,νn∈ℤ  implies that

ν1=ν2=…=νn= 0.

For example, the set of prime numbers is multiplicatively independent, by the fundamental theorem of arithmetics.

Any algebraically independent set is also multiplicatively independent.

Evidently, {x1,x2,…,xn} is multiplicatively independent if and only if the numbers log⁡x1, log⁡x2, …, log⁡xn are linearly independent over ℚ.  Thus the Schanuel’s conjecture may be formulated as the

Conjecture.  If  {x1,x2,…,xn}  is multiplicatively independent, then the transcendence degree of the set

{x1,x2,…,xn,log⁡x1,log⁡x2,…,log⁡xn}

is at least n.
