Anton’s congruence


For every n∈ℕ (n⁢!¯)p stands for the product of numbers between 1 and n which are not divisible by a given prime p. And we set (0⁢!¯)p=1.

The corollary below generalizes a result first found by Anton, Stickelberger, and Hensel:

Let N0 be the least non-negative residue of n(modps) where p is a prime number and n∈ℕ. Then

(n⁢!¯)p≡(±1)⌊n/ps⌋⋅(N0⁢!¯)p(modps).

≡ (ps⁢!¯)p⌊n/ps⌋⋅(N0⁢!¯)p(modps).
