ideal norm


Let α and β be algebraic integers in an algebraic number field K and 𝔪 a non-zero ideal in the ring of integers of K.  We say that α and β are congruent modulo the ideal 𝔪 in the case that  α-β∈𝔪.  This is denoted by

α≡β(mod𝔪).

This congruence relation the ring of integers of K into equivalence classes, which are called the residue classes modulo the ideal 𝔪.

Definition.  Let K be an algebraic number field and  𝔞  a non-zero ideal in K.  The absolute norm of ideal 𝔞 means the number of all residue classes modulo 𝔞.

Remark.  The of any ideal 𝔞 of K is finite — it has the expression

N⁢(𝔞)=Δ⁢(𝔞)d

where Δ⁢(𝔞) is the discriminant of the ideal and d the fundamental number of the field.
