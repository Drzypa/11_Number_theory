Gauss sum


Let p be a prime. Let χ be any multiplicative group character on ℤ/p⁢ℤ (that is, any group homomorphism of multiplicative groups (ℤ/p⁢ℤ)×→ℂ×). For any a∈ℤ/p⁢ℤ, the complex number

ga⁢(χ):=∑t∈ℤ/p⁢ℤχ⁢(t)⁢e2⁢π⁢i⁢a⁢t/p

is called a Gauss sum on ℤ/p⁢ℤ associated to χ.

In general, the equation ga⁢(χ)=χ⁢(a-1)⁢g1⁢(χ) (for nontrivial a and χ) reduces the computation of general Gauss sums to that of g1⁢(χ). The absolute value of g1⁢(χ) is always p as long as χ is nontrivial, and if χ is a quadratic character (that is, χ⁢(t) is the Legendre symbol (tp)), then the value of the Gauss sum is known to be

g1⁢(χ)={p,p≡1(mod4),i⁢p,p≡3(mod4).
