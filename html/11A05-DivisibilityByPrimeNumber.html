divisibility by prime number


Theorem.

Let a and b be integers and p any prime number.  Then we have:

p∣ab ⇔ p∣a∨p∣b (1)

Proof. Suppose that  p∣ab.  Then either  p∣a  or  p∤a.  In the latter case we have  gcd⁡(a,p)=1,  and therefore the corollary of Bézout’s lemma gives the result  p∣b.  Conversely, if  p∣a  or  p∣b,  then for example  a=m⁢p  for some integer m; this implies that  a⁢b=m⁢b⋅p,  i.e.  p∣ab.

Remark 1. The theorem means, that if a product is divisible by a prime number, then at least one of the factor is divisibe by the prime number. Also conversely.

Remark 2. The condition (1) is expressed in of principal ideals as

(a⁢b)⊆(p) ⇔ (a)⊆(p)∨(b)⊆(p). (2)

Here, (p) is a prime ideal of ℤ.
