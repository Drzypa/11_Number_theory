Beatty’s theorem


If p and q are positive irrationals such that

1p+1q=1

then the sequences

{⌊np⌋}n=1∞ = ⌊p⌋,⌊2⁢p⌋,⌊3⁢p⌋,…
{⌊nq⌋}n=1∞ = ⌊q⌋,⌊2⁢q⌋,⌊3⁢q⌋,…

where ⌊x⌋ denotes the floor (or greatest integer function) of x, constitute a partition of the set of positive integers.

That is, every positive integer is a member exactly once of one of the two sequences and the two sequences have no common terms.
