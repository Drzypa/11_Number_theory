sum of reciprocals of Sylvester’s sequence


We will show that the sum of the reciprocals of the Sylvester numbers indeed converges to 1.

Let sn denote a partial sum of the series of reciprocals:

sn=∑i0n-11ai

We would like to show that limn→∞⁡sn=1. Putting over a common denominator, we obtain

sn=∑j=0n-1∏i≠j0≤i<nai∏i=0n-1ai.

Define bn as follows:

bn=1+∑j=0n-1∏i≠j0≤i<nai

Using this new definition and the definition of the Sylvester numbers, we can rewrite the expression for sn as follows:

sn=bn+1an-1

Let us now consider this sequence bn. We will start by deriving a recurrence relation:

bn+1-1 = ∑j=0n∏i≠j0≤i<n+1ai=∏i=0n-1ai+an⁢∑j=0n-1∏i≠j0≤i<nai
= (an-1)+an⁢(bn-1)

Simplifying, we have bn+1=an⁢bn. Now, b2=1+a0+a1=6, hence we can solve the recursion with a product:

bn = b2⁢∏i=2n-1ai
= b2a0⁢a1⁢∏1=0n-1ai
= ∏1=0n-1ai
= an-1

Substituting this in the expression for sn yields

sn=anan-1.

Since limn→∞⁡an=∞, it follows that limn→∞⁡sn=1.
