canonical basis


Let ϑ be an algebraic integer of degree (http://planetmath.org/ExtensionField) n.  The algebraic number field ℚ⁢(ϑ) has always an integral basis of the form

ω1=1,
ω2=a21+ϑd2,
ω3=a31+a32⁢ϑ+ϑ2d3,
⋮   ⋮   ⋮
ωn=an⁢1+an⁢2⁢ϑ+…+an,n-1⁢ϑn-2+ϑn-1dn,

where the ai⁢j’s and di’s are rational integers such that

d2⁢∣d3∣⁢d4⁢∣…∣⁢dn,

i.e.

di∣di+1 ∀i=2, 3,…,n-1.

The integral basis  ω1,ω2,…,ωn is called a canonical basis of the number field.

Remark.  The integers ai⁢j can be reduced so that for all i and j,

-di2<ai⁢j≦di2.

Then one may speak of an adjusted canonical basis.  In the case of a quadratic number field ℚ⁢(d) with  d≡1⁢(mod⁢ 4)  we have (see the examples of ring of integers of a number field)

ω1=1,ω2=1+d2.

The discriminant of this basis is d.
