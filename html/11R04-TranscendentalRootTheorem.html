transcendental root theorem


Suppose a constant x is transcendental over some field F. Then xn is also transcendental over F for any n≥1.

Proof.

Let F¯ denote an algebraic closure of F. Assume for the sake of contradiction that xn∈F¯. Then since algebraic numbers are closed under multiplication (and thus exponentiation by positive integers), we have (xn)n=x∈F¯, so that x is algebraic over F, creating a contradiction. ∎
