near-square prime


A near-square prime is a prime number p of the form n2+k, with n being any integer and 0<|k|<|n| also an integer. Since for any nonzero real number x it is always the case that x2≥0, it doesn’t matter if n is negative.

Fermat primes are near-square primes for k=1 with the additional requirement that n=22m-1, while Carol primes are near-square primes for k=-2 with the additional requirement that n=2m-1.

For k=-1, only n=2 gives a prime, namely 3.
