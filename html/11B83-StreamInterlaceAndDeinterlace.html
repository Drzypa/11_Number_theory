stream interlace and deinterlace


Interlace is the method to create a new data stream from two data streams, each of which has a constant time interval sequence. Formally, suppose A=(a,Δa) and B=(b,Δb) are two data streams, each have a constant time sequence. For convenience, we use Δa and Δb to also denote the constant terms of each of those sequences. We construct a new data stream C=(c,Δc), also having constant time interval, as follows:

cn={bn-⌊n⁢z⌋⌊n⁢z⌋=⌊(n+1)⁢z⌋a⌊n⁢z⌋⌊n⁢z⌋≠⌊(n+1)⁢z⌋,z=ΔbΔa+Δb,Δc=Δa⁢ΔbΔa+Δb

Deinterlace is the method of constructing two data streams , A and B, each having constant time interval, from a given data stream C and primary interlace value Δ of computed stream, where C has constant time interval.

an=cn+⌈(n+1)⁢ΔaΔb⌉,Δa=Δc⁢Δb|Δc-Δb| and bn=cn+⌊n⁢ΔbΔa⌋,Δb=Δc⁢Δa|Δc-Δa|

This sequences are the Fraenkel partition theorem instance.
