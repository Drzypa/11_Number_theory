pure cubic field


A pure cubic field is an extension of ℚ of the form ℚ⁢(n3) for some n∈ℤ such that n3∉ℚ. If n<0, then n3=-|n|3=-|n|3, causing ℚ⁢(n3)=ℚ⁢(|n|3). Thus, without loss of generality, it may be assumed that n>1.

Note that no pure cubic field is Galois (http://planetmath.org/GaloisExtension) over ℚ. For if n∈ℤ is cubefree with |n|≠1, then x3-n is its minimal polynomial over ℚ. This polynomial factors as (x-n3)⁢(x2+x⁢n3+n23) over K=ℚ⁢(|n|3). The discriminant (http://planetmath.org/PolynomialDiscriminant) of x2+x⁢n3+n23 is (n3)2-4⁢(1)⁢(n23)=n23-4⁢n23=-3⁢n23. Since the of x2+x⁢n3+n23 is negative, it does not factor in ℝ. Note that K⊆ℝ. Thus, x3-n has a root (http://planetmath.org/Root) in K but does not split completely in K.

Note also that pure cubic fields are real cubic fields with exactly one real embedding. Thus, a possible method of determining all of the units of pure cubic fields is outlined in the entry regarding units of real cubic fields with exactly one real embedding.
