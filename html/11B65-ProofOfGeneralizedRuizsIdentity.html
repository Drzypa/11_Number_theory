proof of generalized Ruiz’s identity


Theorem.

Consider the polynomials ci,j⁢(x)=(x+i)j-(x+i-1)j. Then, for every positive natural number n,

det⁡(ci,j)i,j=1n=∏k=1nk!
Proof.

Consider the matrices M,C defined by Mi,j=(-1)j⁢(i-1j-1) and Ci,j=(x+i)j-(x+i-1)j.

(M⁢C)i,j =∑k=1n(-1)k⁢(i-1k-1)⁢((x+k)j-(x+k-1)j)
=∑k=0n(-1)k⁢((i-1k-1)+(i-1k))⁢(x+k)j
=∑k=0n(-1)k⁢(ik)⁢(x+k)j
=(-1)j⁢∑k=0i(-1)k⁢(ik)⁢(-x-k)j

Therefore, by Ruiz’s identity, (M⁢C)i,i=(-1)i⁢i! for every i∈{1,…,d} and (M⁢C)i,j=0 for every i,j∈{1,…,n} such that i>j. This means that M⁢C is an upper triangular matrix whose main diagonal is -1!,2!,-3!,…,(-1)n⁢n!. Since the determinant of such a matrix is the product of the elements in the main diagonal, we get that det⁡M⁢C=(-1)n⁢∏k=1nk!. It is easy to see that M itself is lower triangular with determinant (-1)n. Therefore det⁡C=∏k=1nk!. ∎
