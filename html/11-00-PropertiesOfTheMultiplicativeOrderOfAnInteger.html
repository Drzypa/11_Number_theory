properties of the multiplicative order of an integer


Definition.

Let m>1 be an integer and let a be another integer relatively prime to m. The order of a modulo m (or the multiplicative order of amodm) is the smallest positive integer n such that an≡1modm. The order is sometimes denoted by ord⁡a or ordm⁡a.

Proposition.

Let m be a positive integer and suppose that (a,m)=1.

  1. 1.

    as≡1modm if and only if ord⁡a divides s. In particular, ord⁡a divides ϕ⁢(m), where ϕ is the Euler phi function.

  2. 2.

    as≡atmodm if and only if s≡tmodord⁡a.

  3. 3.

    If ord⁡a=d then ord⁡ak=dgcd⁡(k,d) for any k≥1.

  4. 4.

    If ord⁡a=d and e is a positive divisor of d then ad/e has exact order e.

  5. 5.

    Suppose ord⁡a=h and ord⁡b=k with gcd⁡(h,k)=1. Then ord⁡(a⁢b)=h⁢k.
