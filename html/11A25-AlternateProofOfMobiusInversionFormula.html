alternate proof of Möbius inversion formula


The Möbius inversion theorem can also be proved elegantly using the fact that arithmetic functions form a ring under + and *.

Let I be the arithmetic function that is everywhere 1. Then obviously if μ is the Möbius function,

(μ*I)⁢(n)=∑d|nμ⁢(d)⁢I⁢(nd)=∑d|nμ⁢(d)={1n=10otherwise

and thus I*μ=e, where e is the identity of the ring.

But then

f⁢(n)=∑d|ng⁢(d)=∑d|ng⁢(d)⁢I⁢(nd)

and so f=g*I. Thus f*μ=g*I*μ=g. But g=f*μ means precisely that

g⁢(n)=∑d|nμ⁢(d)⁢f⁢(nd)

and we are done.

The reverse equivalence is similar (f*μ=g⇒f*μ*I=g*I⇒f=g*I).
