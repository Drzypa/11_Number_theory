positive definite form


A bilinear form B on a real or complex vector space V is positive definite if B⁢(x,x)>0 for all nonzero vectors x∈V. On the other hand, if B⁢(x,x)<0 for all nonzero vectors x∈V, then we say B is negative definite. If B⁢(x,x)≥0 for all vectors x∈V, then we say B is nonnegative definite. Likewise, if B⁢(x,x)≤0 for all vectors x∈V, then we say B is nonpositive definite.

A form which is neither positive definite nor negative definite is called indefinite.
