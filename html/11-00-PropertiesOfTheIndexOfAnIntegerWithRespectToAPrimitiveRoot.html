properties of the index of an integer with respect to a primitive root


Definition.

Let m>1 be an integer such that the integer g is a primitive root for m. Suppose a is another integer relatively prime to g. The index of a (to base g) is the smallest positive integer n such that gn≡amodm, and it is denoted by ind⁡a or indg⁡a.

Proposition.

Suppose g is a primitive root of m.

  1. 1.

    ind⁡1≡0modϕ⁢(m); ind⁡g≡1modϕ⁢(m), where ϕ is the Euler phi function.

  2. 2.

    a≡bmodm if and only if ind⁡a≡ind⁡bmodϕ⁢(m).

  3. 3.

    ind⁡(a⁢b)≡ind⁡a+ind⁡bmodϕ⁢(m).

  4. 4.

    ind⁡ak≡k⁢ind⁡amodϕ⁢(m) for any k≥0.
