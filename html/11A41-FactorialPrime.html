factorial prime


A factorial prime is a number that is one less or one more than a factorial and is also a prime number. The first few factorial primes are: 2, 3, 5, 7, 23, 719, 5039, 39916801, 479001599, 87178291199 (sequence A088054 in the OEIS). It is conjectured that only for n=3 are both n!-1 and n!+1 both primes.

If n is a positive integer and p is a prime number, n!+p is never a prime for p<n, because obviously it will be a multiple of p, just as n! is. But n!+1, even though it certainly is a multiple of 1, can be a prime, specifically, a factorial prime. (The same is also true if we subtract instead of add).
