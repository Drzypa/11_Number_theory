divisibility


Given integers a and b, then we say a divides b if and only if there is some q∈ℤ such that b=q⁢a.

There are many other ways in common use to express this relationship:

  • •

    a∣b (read “a divides b”).

  • •

    b is divisible by a.

  • •

    a is a factor of b.

  • •

    a is a divisor of b.

  • •

    b is a multiple of a.

The notion of divisibility can apply to other rings (e.g., polynomials).
