independence of characteristic polynomial on primitive element


The simple field extension ℚ⁢(ϑ)/ℚ where ϑ is an algebraic number of degree (http://planetmath.org/DegreeOfAnAlgebraicNumber) n may be determined also by using another primitive element η.  Then we have

η∈ℚ⁢(ϑ),

whence, by the entry degree of algebraic number, the degree of η divides the degree of ϑ.  But also

ϑ∈ℚ⁢(η),

whence the degree of ϑ divides the degree of η.  Therefore any possible primitive element of the field extension has the same degree n.  This number is the degree of the number field (http://planetmath.org/NumberField), i.e. the degree of the field extension, as comes clear from the entry canonical form of element of number field.

Although the characteristic polynomial

g⁢(x):=∏i=1n[x-r⁢(ϑi)]=∏i=1n(x-α(i))

of an element α of the algebraic number field ℚ⁢(ϑ) is based on the primitive element ϑ, the equation

g⁢(x)=(x-α1)m⁢(x-α2)m⁢⋯⁢(x-αk)m (1)

in the entry http://planetmath.org/node/12050degree of algebraic number shows that the polynomial is fully determined by the algebraic conjugates of α itself and the number m which equals the degree n divided by the degree k of α.

The above stated makes meaningful to define the norm and the trace functions in an algebraic number field as follows.

Definition.  If α is an element of the number field ℚ⁢(ϑ), then the norm N⁢(α) and the trace S⁢(α) of α are the product and the sum, respectively, of all http://planetmath.org/node/12046ℚ⁢(ϑ)-conjugates α(i) of α.

Since the coefficients of the characteristic equation of α are rational, one has

N:ℚ⁢(ϑ)→ℚ and S:ℚ⁢(ϑ)→ℚ.

In fact, one can infer from (1) that

N⁢(α)=akm,S⁢(α)=-m⁢a1, (2)

where xk+a1⁢xk-1+…+ak is the minimal polynomial of α.
