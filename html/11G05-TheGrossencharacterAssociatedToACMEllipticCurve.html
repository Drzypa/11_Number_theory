the Grössencharacter associated to a CM elliptic curve


Let K be a quadratic imaginary field and let A/F be an elliptic curve defined over a number field F (such that K⊂F), with complex multiplication by K. The so-called ‘Main Theorem of Complex Multiplication’ ([2], Thm. 8.2) implies the existence of a Grössencharacter of F, ψA/F:𝒜F∗→ℂ∗ associated to the curve A/F satisfying several interesting properties which we collect in the following statement.

Theorem ([2], Thm. 9.1, Prop. 10.4, Cor. 10.4.1).

Let ℘ be a prime of F of good reduction for A/F, i.e. the reduction A~/F of A modulo ℘ is smooth. There exists a Grössencharacter of F, ψA/F:AF∗→C∗, such that:

  1. 1.

    ψA/F is unramified at a prime 𝔔 of F if and only if A/F has good reduction at 𝔔;

  2. 2.

    ψA/F⁢(℘) belongs to 𝒪K, thus multiplication by [ψA/F⁢(℘)] is a well defined endomorphism of A/F. Moreover NℚF⁢(℘)=NℚK⁢(ψA/F⁢(℘));

  3. 3.

    the following diagram is commutative

    \xymatrix⁢A⁢\ar⁢@->[d]⁢\ar⁢@->[r][ψA/F⁢(℘)]⁢&⁢A⁢\ar⁢@->[d]⁢&⁢A~⁢\ar⁢@->[r]ϕ℘⁢&⁢A~⁢&

    where ϕ℘:A~→A~ be the NℚF⁢(℘)-power Frobenius map and the vertical maps are reduction mod ℘;

  4. 4.

    let |A~⁢(𝒪F/℘)| be the number of points in A~ over the finite field 𝒪F/℘ and put a℘=NℚF⁢(℘)+1-|A~⁢(𝒪F/℘)|. Then

    a℘=ψA/F⁢(℘)+ψA/F⁢(℘)¯=2⋅ℜ⁡(ψA/F⁢(℘)).
  5. 5.

    (due to Deuring) let L⁢(A/F,s) be the L-function associated to the elliptic curve A/F. If K⊂F then L⁢(A/F,s)=L⁢(ψA/F,s)⁢L⁢(ψA/F¯,s). If K⊈F, and F′=F⁢K, then L⁢(E/F,s)=L⁢(ψA/F′,s).

In particular, if hK=1 then A is defined over K (actually, it may be defined over ℚ), ψA/K⁢(℘) is a generator of ℘ (by part (2), and the explicit generator can be pinned down using part (4)). Thus, if e is the number of roots of unity in K, then ψA/Kk⁢(℘)=αk where α is any generator of ℘. Also, by part (5), L⁢(A/ℚ,s)=L⁢(ψA/K,s).
