class number divisibility in cyclic extensions


In this entry, the class number of a number field L is denoted by hL.

Theorem 1.

Let F/K be a cyclic Galois extension of degree n. Let p be a prime such that n is not divisible by p, and assume that p does not divide hE, the class number of any intermediate field K⊆E⊊F. If p divides hF then pf also divides hF, where f is the multiplicative order of p modulo n.

Recall that the multiplicative order of p modulo n is a number f such that pf≡1modn and pm is not congruent to 1 modulo n for any 1≤m<f.

Corollary 1.

Let F/K be a Galois extension such that [F:K]=q is a prime distinct from the prime p. Assume that p does not divide hK. If p divides hF then pf divides hF, where f is the multiplicative order of p modulo q.

Note that a Galois extension F/K of prime degree has no non-trivial subextensions.
