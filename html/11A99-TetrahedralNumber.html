tetrahedral number


An integer of the form

(n2+n)⁢(n+2)6,

where n is a nonnegative integer. Sometimes referred to as Tn, tetrahedral numbers are listed in A000292 of Sloane’s OEIS. 2|Tn except when n≡1mod4.

With tn the nth triangular number, the nth tetrahedral number can be calculated with this formula:

Tn=∑i=1nti.

Another way to calculate tetrahedral numbers is with the binomial coefficient

Tn=(n+23).

This means that tetrahedral numbers can be looked up in Pascal’s triangle.

Tetrahedral numbers have practical applications in sphere packing.
