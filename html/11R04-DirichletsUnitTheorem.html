Dirichlet’s unit theorem


\PMlinkescapephrase

occur in

Let K be a number field, and let 𝒪K be its ring of integers. Then

𝒪K*≅μ⁢(K)×ℤr+s-1.

Here 𝒪K* is the group of units of 𝒪K, μ⁢(K) is the finite cyclic group of the roots of unity in 𝒪K*, r is the number of real embeddings K→ℝ, and 2⁢s is the number of non-real complex embeddings K→ℂ (which occur in complex conjugate pairs, so s is an integer).
