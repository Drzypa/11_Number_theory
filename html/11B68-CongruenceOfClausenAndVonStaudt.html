congruence of Clausen and von Staudt


Let Bk denote the kth Bernoulli number:

B0=1,B1=-12,B2=16,B3=0,B4=-130,…

In fact, Bk=0 for all odd k≥3, so we will only consider Bk for even k. The following is a well-known congruence, due to Thomas Clausen and Karl von Staudt.

Theorem (Congruence of Clausen and von Staudt).

For an even integer k≥2,

Bk≡-∑p prime,(p-1)|k1pmodℤ

where the sum is over all primes p such that (p-1) divides k. In other words, there exists an integer nk such that

Bk=nk-∑p prime,(p-1)|k1p.

For example:

B2=16=1-12-13,B4=-130=1-12-13-15.

Sometimes the theorem is stated in this alternative form:

Corollary.

For an even integer k≥2 and any prime p the product p⁢Bk is p-integral, that is, p⁢Bk is a rational number t/s (in lowest terms) such that p does not divide s. Moreover:

p⁢Bk≡{-1modp, if (p-1) divides k;0modp, if (p-1) does not divide k.
