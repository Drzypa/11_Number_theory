Andrica’s conjecture


Conjecture (Dorin Andrica). Given the nth prime pn, it is always the case that 1>pn+1-pn.

This conjecture remains unproven as of 2007. The conjecture has been checked up to n=105 by computers.

The largest known difference of square roots of consecutive primes happens for the small n=4, being approximately 0.67087347929081. The difference of the square roots of the primes 10314-1929 and 10314+2318 (which cap a prime gap of 4247 consecutive composite numbers discovered in 1992 by Baugh & O’Hara) is a very small number which is obviously much smaller than 1.
