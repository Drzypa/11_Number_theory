the j-invariant classifies elliptic curves up to isomorphism


In this entry, an isomorphism over K should be understood in the sense of the entry isomorphism of varieties.

Theorem 1.

Let K be a field, and let K¯ be a fixed algebraic closure of K.

  1. 1.

    Two elliptic curves E1 and E2 are isomorphic (http://planetmath.org/IsomorphismOfVarieties) (over K¯) if and only if they have the same j-invariant, i.e. j⁢(E1)=j⁢(E2).

  2. 2.

    Let j0∈K¯ be fixed. There exists an elliptic curve E defined over the field K⁢(j0) such that j⁢(E)=j0.

Proof.

For part 2:

  • •

    For j0=0, the curve E0:y2+y=x3 satisfies j⁢(E)=0;

  • •

    For j0=1728, the curve E1728:y2=x3+x satisfies j⁢(E1728)=1728;

  • •

    If j0≠0,1728 consider the elliptic curve:

    E=Ej0:y2+x⁢y=x3-36j0-1728⁢x-1j0-1728.

    It satisfies j⁢(E)=j0 and it is defined over K⁢(j0).

∎
