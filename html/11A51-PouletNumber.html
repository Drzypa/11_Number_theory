Poulet number


A Poulet number or Sarrus number is a composite integer n such that 2n≡2modn. In other words, a base 2 pseudoprime (thus a Poulet number that satisfies the congruence for other bases is a Carmichael number). The first few Poulet numbers are 341, 561, 645, 1105, 1387, 1729, 1905, listed in A001567 of Sloane’s OEIS.

For example, 561 is a Poulet number, since 2561-2 is 75479248496430827044831091619765377 81833842440832880856752412600491248324784297704172253450355317535082936750061527 689799541169259849585265122868502865392087298790653950 and that’s divisible by 561. The number 561 is not prime, it has the prime factors 3, 11, and 17.

Poulet numbers are counterexamples to the Chinese hypothesis.
