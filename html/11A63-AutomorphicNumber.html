automorphic number


Given a base b integer

n=∑i=1kdi⁢bi-1

where d1 is the least significant digit and dk is the most significant, if it’s also the case that the k least significant digits of m⁢n2 are the same of those of n, then n is called an automorphic number, or an m-automorphic number.

Neither b nor b+1 can be 1-automorphic in base b.
