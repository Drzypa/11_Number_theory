proof of Minkowski’s bound


The proof of Minkowski’s bound will rely on Minkowski’s lattice point theorem (http://planetmath.org/MinkowskisTheorem), but we first need to establish some lemmas.

Lemma 1.

Let M be a real number and suppose that for every non-zero ideal a of the ring of integers OK there exists a non-zero x∈a with norm N⁡(x)≤M⁢N⁡(a).

Then, every ideal class of OK has a representative a satisfying N⁡(a)≤M.

Proof.

Let [𝔟] be an ideal class represented by the ideal 𝔟. Choosing a non-zero x∈𝔟 then x⁢𝔟-1 is an ideal of 𝒪K and, by the condition of the lemma, contains a non-zero y satisfying N⁡(y)≤M⁢N⁡(x⁢𝔟-1). Then, 𝔞≡x-1⁢y⁢𝔟 is an ideal representing [𝔟] and N⁡(𝔞)=N⁡(y)/N⁡(x⁢𝔟-1)≤M. ∎

If the real embeddings of K are denoted by σk:K→ℝ (k=1,…,r1) and the complex embeddings are τk:K→ℂ together with their complex conjugates τ¯k (k=1,…,r2), then we define

j:K→ℝr1×ℂr2,
j⁢(x)=(σ1⁢(x),…,σr1⁢(x),τ1⁢(x),…,τr2⁢(x)).

Also note that ℝr1×ℂr2 is isomorphic as a real vector space to ℝr1+2⁢r2=ℝn given by the isomorphism

f:ℝr1×ℂr2→ℝn,
f⁢(x1,…,xr1,y1,…,yr2)=(x1,…,xr1,ℜ⁡(y1),…,ℜ⁡(yr2),ℑ⁡(y1),…,ℑ⁡(yr2)).

As f and j are linear maps (with respect to the field of rationals ℚ), the combination f∘j gives a ℚ-linear map from K to ℝn. The image will be a lattice, and we can compute its volume.

Lemma 2.

If a is a non-zero ideal of OK, then Γ=f∘j⁢(a) is a lattice in Rn (http://planetmath.org/LatticeInMathbbRn). Its fundamental mesh has volume

vol⁡(Γ)=2-r2⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞).

Lemma 3.

For any L>0, let S be the set in Rr1×Cr2 consisting of points (x1,…,xr1,y1,yr2) satisfying

∑k=1r1|xk|+2⁢∑k=1r2|yk|≤L.

Then, f⁢(S) has volume (2r1-r2⁢πr2/n!)⁢Ln.

Proof of Minkowski’s bound

For an ideal 𝔞 and any constant b>1, let L>0 be given by

2r1-r2⁢πr2n!⁢Ln=2n⁢b⁢2-r2⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞).

Letting S be the set given in Lemma 3 and Γ=f∘j⁢(𝔞), Lemmas 2 and 3 give vol⁡(S)>2n⁢vol⁡(Γ). As S is convex and symmetric about the origin, Minkowski’s theorem tells us that there is a non-zero x∈𝔞 with f∘j⁢(x)∈S.

As the geometric mean is always bounded above by the arithmetic mean, we get the inequality

N⁡(x)=∏k=1r1|σk⁢(x)|⁢∏k=1r2|τk⁢(x)|2≤n-n⁢(∑k=1r1|σk⁢(x)|+2⁢∑k=1r2|τk⁢(x)|)n≤n-n⁢Ln=b⁢MK⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞)

where MK=(n!/nn)⁢(4/π)r2. If we choose b such that b⁢MK⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞) is less than the smallest integer greater than MK⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞), then this gives N⁡(x)≤MK⁢|DK|⁢N⁡(𝔞) and Minkowski’s bound follows from Lemma 1.
