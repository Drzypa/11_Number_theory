Riemann-Hurwitz theorem


First we define the different divisor of an extension of function fields. Let K be a function field over a field F and let L be a finite separable extension of K. Let 𝒪P be a prime of K, i.e. a discrete valuation ring with F⊂𝒪P, maximal ideal P and quotient field equal to K. Let RP be the integral closure of 𝒪P in L. Notice that if 𝔭 is a prime ideal of RP, then the localization 𝒪𝔭=(RP)𝔭 is a prime of L (which is said to be lying over 𝒪P). The maximal ideal of 𝒪𝔭 is 𝔭⁢(RP)𝔭.

Let 𝒪𝔓 be any prime of L, then it lays over some prime ideal P of K and in fact, if 𝔭=RP∩𝔓 then 𝒪𝔭≅𝒪𝔓. Let δ⁢(𝔓) be the exact power of 𝔭 dividing the different of RP over 𝒪P (the different of an extension of Dedekind domains is a fractional ideal). We define the different divisor of L/K as follows:

DL/K=∑𝔓δ⁢(𝔓)⁢𝔓

as an element of the free abelian group generated by the prime ideals of L.

Theorem (Riemann-Hurwitz).

Let L/K be a finite, separable, geometric extension of function fields and suppose the genus of K is gK. Then the genus of L is given by the formula:

2gL-2=[L:K](2gK-2)+degLDL/K.
