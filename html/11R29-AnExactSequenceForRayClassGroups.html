an exact sequence for ray class groups


Let K be a number field, let 𝒪K be its ring of integers and let 𝔪 be a modulus in K, i.e.

𝔪=𝔪0⁢𝔪∞

where 𝔪0 is an integral ideal in 𝒪K and 𝔪∞ is a product of real infinite places (i.e. real archimedean primes). Let Cl⁡(K) be the ideal class group of K and let Cl⁡(K,𝔪) be the ray class group of K of conductor 𝔪. Also, define

(𝒪K/𝔪)×=(𝒪K/𝔪0)××(ℤ/2⁢ℤ)|𝔪∞|

where |𝔪∞| denotes the number of real places in 𝔪. Finally, let U=𝒪K× be the unit group of K.

Proposition.

The elements above fit in the following exact sequence:

U⟶(𝒪K/𝔪)×⟶Cl⁡(K,𝔪)⟶Cl⁡(K)⟶1.
Example 1.

Let K=ℚ. Thus, Cl⁡(ℚ) is trivial and U={±1}≅ℤ/2⁢ℤ. Let 𝔪=p⁢∞ where p>2 is any prime. Then:

(ℤ/𝔪)×=(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)××(ℤ/2⁢ℤ).

The exact sequence now reads:

ℤ/2⁢ℤ⟶(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)××(ℤ/2⁢ℤ)⟶Cl⁡(ℚ,p⁢∞)⟶1.

Therefore, Cl⁡(ℚ,p⁢∞)≅(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)×. In fact, as we know, the http://planetmath.org/node/RayClassFieldray class field of ℚ of conductor 𝔪=p⁢∞ is the cyclotomic field ℚ⁢(ζp) where ζp is any primitive pth root of unity. Moreover

Gal⁡(ℚ⁢(ζp)/ℚ)≅Cl⁡(ℚ,p⁢∞)≅(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)×.

Finally notice that the ray class group of ℚ of conductor 𝔪=p is simply (ℤ/p⁢ℤ)×/{±1} which corresponds to the ray class field ℚ⁢(ζp)+=ℚ⁢(ζp+ζp-1), the maximal real subfield of ℚ⁢(ζp).
