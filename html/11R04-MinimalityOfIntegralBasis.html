minimality of integral basis


The discriminant  Δ:=Δ⁢(α1,α2,…,αs)  of the set  {α1,α2,…,αs}  of integers of an algebraic number field K is a rational integer.  If this set is an integral basis of K, then |Δ| has the least possible (positive integer) value in the field K, and conversely.  The value  d=Δ  is equal for all integral bases of K, and it is called the discriminant or fundamental number of the field.
