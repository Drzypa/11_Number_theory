discrete logarithm


Let p be a prime. We know that the group G:=(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)* is cyclic. Let g be a primitive root of G, i.e. G={1,g,g2,…,gp-2}. For a number x∈G we want to know the unique number 0≤n≤p-2 with

x=gn.

This number n is called the discrete logarithm or index of x to the basis g and is denoted as indg⁡(x). For x,y∈G it satisfies the following properties:

indg⁡(x⁢y) =indg⁡(x)+indg⁡(y);
indg⁡(x-1) =-indg⁡(x);
indg⁡(xk) =k⋅indg⁡(x).

Furthermore, for a pair g,h of distinct primitive roots, we also have, for any x∈G:

indh⁡(x) =indh⁡(g)⋅indg⁡(x);
1 =indg⁡(h)⋅indh⁡(g);
indg⁡(-1) =p-12.

It is a difficult problem to compute the discrete logarithm, while powering is very easy. Therefore this is of some interest to cryptography.
