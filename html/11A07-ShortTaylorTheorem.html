short Taylor theorem


If f⁢(x) is a polynomial with integer coefficients and x0 and h integers, then the congruence

f⁢(x0+h)≡f⁢(x0)+f′⁢(x0)⁢h(modh2) (1)

is in force.

Proof.  Because of the linear properties of (1) we can confine us to the monomials  f⁢(x):=xn.  Then  f′⁢(x)=n⁢xn-1.  By the binomial theorem we have

(x0+h)n=x0n+n⁢x0n-1⁢h+h2⁢P⁢(x0) (2)

where P⁢(x0) is a polynomial in x0 with integer coefficients.  The equality (2) may be written as the asserted congruence (1).
