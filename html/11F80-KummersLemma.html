Kummer’s lemma


The following result is a key ingredient in the proof of Fermat’s last theorem for regular primes. More concretely, the lemma is needed to show the so-called second case of Fermat, i.e. xp+yp=zp does not have any non-trivial solutions in ℤ with p>2 a regular prime and p|xyz. It is due to Ernst Kummer, thus the name.

Theorem (Kummer’s Lemma).

Let p>2 be a prime, let ζp be a primitive pth root of unity and let K=Q⁢(ζp) be the corresponding cyclotomic field. Let E be the group of algebraic units of the ring of integers OK. Suppose that p is a regular prime. If a unit ϵ∈E is congruent modulo p to a rational integer, then ϵ is the pth power of another unit also E.

For a proof, see [Washington], Theorem 5.36. The reader may also be interested in generalizations due to [Washington 1992] and [Ozaki 1997].
