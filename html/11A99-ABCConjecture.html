ABC conjecture


The ABC conjecture states that given any ϵ>0, there is a constant κ⁢(ϵ) such that

max⁡(|A|,|B|,|C|)≤κ⁢(ϵ)⁢(rad⁡(A⁢B⁢C))1+ϵ

for all mutually coprime integers A, B, C with A+B=C, where rad is the radical of an integer. This conjecture was formulated by Masser and Oesterlé in 1980.

The ABC conjecture is considered one of the most important unsolved problems in number , as many results would follow directly from this conjecture. For example, Fermat’s Last Theorem could be proved (for sufficiently large exponents) with about one page worth of proof.
