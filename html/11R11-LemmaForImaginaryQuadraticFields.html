lemma for imaginary quadratic fields


For determining the imaginary quadratic fields whose ring of integers has unique factorization, one can use the following

Lemma.  Let d be a negative integer with  d≡1(mod4),  p the greatest odd irreducible (http://planetmath.org/Irreducible) integer with  p≦13⁢|d|  and  q=14⁢(1-d).  In the imaginary quadratic field ℚ⁢(d), the factorization of integers is unique (http://planetmath.org/Ufd) if and only if the integers

t2-t+q  (t=1, 2,…,p+12) (1)

are irreducible (http://planetmath.org/Irreducible) in the field of the rational numbers.

The lemma yields the below table:

q d=1-4⁢q p 12⁢(p+1) the numbers (1)
1 -3 1 1 1
2 -7 1 1 2
3 -11 1 1 3
5 -19 1 1 5
11 -43 3 2 11, 13
17 -67 3 2 17, 19
41 -163 7 4 41, 43, 47, 53
