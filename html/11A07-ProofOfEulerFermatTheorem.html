proof of Euler-Fermat theorem


Let a1,a2,…,aϕ⁢(n) be all positive integers less than n which are coprime to n. Since gcd⁢(a,n)=1, then the set a⁢a1,a⁢a2,…,a⁢aϕ⁢(n) are each congruent to one of the integers a1,a2,…,aϕ⁢(n) in some order. Taking the product of these congruences, we get

(a⁢a1)⁢(a⁢a2)⁢⋯⁢(a⁢aϕ⁢(n))≡a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢aϕ⁢(n)(modn)

hence

aϕ⁢(n)⁢(a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢aϕ⁢(n))≡a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢aϕ⁢(n)(modn).

Since gcd⁢(a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢aϕ⁢(n),n)=1, we can divide both sides by a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢aϕ⁢(n), and the desired result follows.
