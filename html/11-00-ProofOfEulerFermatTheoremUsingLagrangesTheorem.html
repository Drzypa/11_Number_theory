proof of Euler-Fermat theorem using Lagrange’s theorem


Theorem.

Given a,n∈Z, aϕ⁢(n)≡1(modn) when 𝑔𝑐𝑑⁢(a,n)=1, where ϕ is the Euler totient function.

Proof.

We will make use of Lagrange’s Theorem: Let G be a finite group and let H be a subgroup of G. Then the order of H divides the order of G.

Let G=(ℤ/n⁢ℤ)× and let H be the multiplicative subgroup of G generated by a (so H={1,a,a2,…}). The fact that gcd⁡(a,n)=1 ensures that a∈G. Notice that the order of H, h=|H| is also the order of a, i.e. the smallest natural number n>1 such that an is the identity in G, i.e. ah≡1modn. Also, recall that the order of G=(ℤ/n⁢ℤ)× is ϕ⁢(n), where ϕ is the Euler ϕ function.

By Lagrange’s theorem h∣|G|=ϕ(n), so ϕ⁢(n)=h⋅m for some m. Thus:

aϕ⁢(n)=(ah)m≡1m≡1modn

as claimed. ∎
