Let $\Gamma$ denote the gamma function, $\zeta$ the Riemann zeta function and $s$ any complex number.  Then

\begin{align*}
\pi^\frac{s-1}{2}\Gamma\left(\frac{1-s}{2}\right)\zeta(1-s)=\pi^{\frac{-s}{2}}\Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2}\right)\zeta(s).
\end{align*}

Though the equation appears too intricate to be of any use, the inherent \PMlinkescapetext{symmetry} in the formula makes this the simplest method of evaluating $\zeta(s)$ at points to the left of the critical strip.
